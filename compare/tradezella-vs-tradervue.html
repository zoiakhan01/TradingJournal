1. AI & Analytics
TradeZella Wins
TradeZella
9.5

TradeZella offers 50+ analytics reports, an AI-powered analytics engine that surfaces patterns across your trading data, identifies behavioral tendencies, flags risk concentrations, and highlights your best and worst performing setups. On top of that, 50+ pre-built reports cover every angle: time-of-day performance, day-of-week analysis, R-multiple distribution, custom tag analytics, and more. The Advanced Global Filter lets you slice all reports by any criteria simultaneously. Tradervue provides basic P&L statistics, win-rate calculations, and simple performance charts. The Silver plan ($29.95/mo) adds more detailed reports and filtering, but there's no AI layer, no pattern detection, and no behavioral analysis. For traders who want data to surface actionable insights automatically, TradeZella is in a different category entirely.
2. Backtesting
TradeZella Wins
TradeZella
9.5
Tradervue
1.0
TradeZella includes a full interactive backtesting engine on all plans with unlimited sessions. Test strategies against 11+ years of historical data (back to September 2014) across stocks, futures, forex, and crypto. Features include multi-chart layouts (up to 8 charts), multi-symbol support (up to 5 per session), built-in ICT indicators (FVG, Asian Session Range, HTF Bias, Key Levels, Power of 3), realistic order execution with market/limit/stop orders, auto-breakeven, partial closes, speed control (0.5x–10x), and every backtested trade auto-logged to your journal. Tradervue has no backtesting functionality whatsoever. No historical data access, no strategy testing, no simulation tools. For traders who want to test ideas before risking real capital, this is a dealbreaker.
3. Trade Replay
TradeZella Wins
TradeZella
9.5
Tradervue
1.0
TradeZella includes Trade Replay on all plans with three distinct modes. Trade Replay lets you rewatch individual trades second by second. Day Replay lets you replay your entire trading session to understand how trades interacted. Scenario Replay (coming soon) will add scenario-based review. Controls include adjustable speed (1x–30x), entry/exit markers, multiple timeframe views, Time of Sales data, and Level II market depth. You can tag mistakes and add notes during replay, with everything syncing to your analytics. Tradervue has no trade replay at all. No chart playback, no session review, no interactive tools for rewatching trades. You can view trade history in a table and attach manual notes, but that's it.

Strategy analytics show win rate, net P&L, R-multiple, and more for each strategy. Tradervue has basic tagging and the ability to categorize trades, but no strategy framework, no templates, no performance tracking per strategy, and no ability to document or share trading approaches.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is TradeZella better than Tradervue for day trading?

Yes. TradeZella offers trade replay, backtesting with 11+ years of data, 50+ analytics reports, and strategy templates, all essential tools for active day traders. Tradervue provides basic trade logging and simple P&L charts but lacks replay, backtesting, and advanced analytics. For day traders who want to review executions, test strategies, and identify patterns, TradeZella is the stronger platform.

Does Tradervue have a free plan?

Yes. Tradervue offers a free tier that allows up to 30 trades per month with basic import and community access. This is Tradervue's main advantage for casual or low-frequency traders who just need simple trade record-keeping at no cost.

How many brokers does TradeZella support vs Tradervue?

TradeZella supports 500+ broker integrations including stocks, options, futures, forex, and crypto. Tradervue supports approximately 80+ brokers covering stocks, options, futures, and forex but does not support cryptocurrency trading. TradeZella covers over 6x more brokers and platforms.

Which trading journal has backtesting: TradeZella or Tradervue?

Only TradeZella offers backtesting. It includes unlimited backtesting on all plans with 11+ years of historical data, multi-chart layouts, and built-in ICT indicators. Tradervue does not have any backtesting functionality.

What is the Trustpilot rating for TradeZella vs Tradervue?

TradeZella has a 4.8/5 rating on Trustpilot, while Tradervue has a 2.6/5 rating. The significant gap reflects differences in feature development, user experience, and ongoing platform investment.

Does TradeZella or Tradervue have trade replay?

TradeZella offers trade replay with three modes: Trade Replay, Day Replay, and Scenario Replay (coming soon). This lets traders visually review their entries and exits on price charts. Tradervue does not offer trade replay in any form.

How much does TradeZella cost compared to Tradervue?

TradeZella starts at $29/month (Essential plan). Tradervue's paid plans are $29.95/month (Silver) and $49.95/month (Gold). While similarly priced, TradeZella includes backtesting, trade replay, strategy templates, and 50+ reports on all plans, features Tradervue doesn't offer at any price tier.
