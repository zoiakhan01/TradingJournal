We Analyzed 1,201 Trustpilot Reviews Across 5 Trading Journals: Here's What Users Actually Say

March 3, 2026 · 12 min read · By the TradingJournal.com team

We read every Trustpilot review for the five major trading journal platforms (TradeZella, TraderSync, Edgewonk, Trademetria, and Tradervue) then categorized every complaint and every piece of praise into recurring themes.

The headline numbers: 91% of TradeZella users rate it 5 stars. 91% of TraderSync users do the same. Meanwhile, 89% of Tradervue's reviews are 1-star, the widest satisfaction gap in the trading journal industry. Here's the full breakdown of what traders actually love, what they hate, and which companies listen.

See our full reviews: TradeZella  |  TraderSync  |  Edgewonk  |  Trademetria  |  Tradervue

Key Findings
  • TradeZella has the highest Trustpilot rating: 4.8/5 from 835 reviews, with 91% five-star ratings. Customer support is praised in 93% of positive reviews.
  • TraderSync rates 4.7/5 from 312 reviews, also with 91% five-star. Support is praised in 75% of positive reviews.
  • Edgewonk rates 4.7/5 from 40 reviews with 97% five-star, but the small sample size makes this less statistically reliable.
  • Tradervue rates 2.6/5 from 9 reviews. 89% of reviews are 1-star, with 88% of complaints citing billing and cancellation issues.
  • Trademetria rates 4.0/5 from only 5 reviews, too few for meaningful analysis.

Methodology

We collected all 1,201 Trustpilot reviews across the five platforms as of March 2026. For negative reviews (1–2 stars), we read and categorized every single one. For positive reviews (5 stars), we sampled the first 40 reviews per platform (or all reviews if fewer than 40 existed). Each review was assigned to one or more theme categories. Percentages reflect how often a theme appears across the reviews in that group, so a single review can be counted in multiple categories.

With only 5 reviews (4 five-star, 1 four-star), Trademetria's sample is too small for meaningful theme analysis. Reviewers mention a clean dashboard, good customer service, and competitive pricing. One reviewer noted the price was “a little on the high side” for their region (India).

Tradervue: What Positive Reviews Say

Community-driven journal with trade sharing features
★ 2.6

Tradervue's single positive review praises the analytics depth and fast trade importing (499 trades uploaded in under 10 minutes). The reviewer credits SMB Capital's endorsement for their decision.

Tradervue was one of the earliest trading journal platforms and has a legacy following among institutional and prop firm traders. Its community sharing features (the ability to publish trades publicly and receive peer feedback) remain unique in the space. However, the platform has not kept pace with modern competitors in terms of AI features, backtesting, or trade replay, which may explain why positive reviews are rare on Trustpilot. Most of Tradervue's reputation exists outside Trustpilot, in trading forums and prop firm communities where it was historically recommended.

Industry Pattern

Customer support is the #1 praised theme for both TradeZella (93%) and TraderSync (75%). Edgewonk breaks the pattern with analytics leading at 65%. This suggests that for the two largest platforms, support quality may be a bigger driver of Trustpilot ratings than product features.

Key Takeaways

  1. TradeZella and TraderSync both hold 91% five-star rates, but TradeZella's comes from a 2.7× larger review pool (835 vs. 312), making it the more statistically confident data point.
  2. Customer support drives ratings more than features. 93% of TradeZella's and 75% of TraderSync's positive reviews praise support. Edgewonk is the exception, where analytics leads at 65%.
  3. Bugs are the universal complaint. Technical issues are the #1 complaint for both TradeZella (37%) and TraderSync (36%). Every trading journal platform struggles with software reliability to some degree.
  4. Tradervue has a billing crisis. 88% of its negative reviews cite billing or cancellation problems. Users report being charged after cancelling, no way to remove payment info, and multi-year unauthorized charges. This is the most concentrated complaint pattern across all five platforms.
  5. Review volume matters. Edgewonk's 97% five-star rate sounds better than TradeZella's 91%, until you realize it comes from 40 reviews vs. 835. Trademetria's 5 reviews and Tradervue's 9 are too small for reliable conclusions.

Methodology Note

All data was collected from Trustpilot.com in March 2026. We read the full text of every negative review (1–2 stars) for each platform, totaling 64 negative reviews. For positive reviews (5 stars), we sampled up to 40 per platform, totaling approximately 141 reviews read in full. Each review was categorized into one or more complaint or praise themes. Reviews mentioning multiple themes were counted in each applicable category. Percentages represent the share of reviews mentioning that theme.

Trustpilot ratings can be influenced by review solicitation practices. Platforms that actively request reviews tend to accumulate higher volumes of positive feedback. This analysis reports the data as-is without adjusting for solicitation bias. All Trustpilot data is publicly available at trustpilot.com.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do trading journal companies respond to negative reviews?

Company response rates to negative reviews are low across the industry. TraderSync responds to approximately 7% of negative reviews, TradeZella to roughly 5%, while Tradervue and Edgewonk show 0% response rates on Trustpilot. No platform has a strong public response strategy.

Is Edgewonk's 97% five-star rate reliable?

Edgewonk has 39 five-star reviews out of 40 total, giving it a 97% five-star rate. However, the small sample size (40 total reviews) means this rate is less statistically reliable than TradeZella's 91% from 835 reviews. A few additional negative reviews could significantly change Edgewonk's average.

Can Trustpilot reviews be faked or manipulated?

Trustpilot has automated fraud detection systems that flag suspicious review patterns, such as clusters of reviews from the same IP address or accounts created solely to leave a single review. However, no system is foolproof. Companies can incentivize reviews (e.g., asking satisfied customers to leave feedback), which tends to inflate positive review volume without technically being fake. In our analysis, we found no evidence of fabricated reviews across the five platforms, but we did note that TradeZella and TraderSync appear to actively encourage Trustpilot reviews, which contributes to their higher review counts (835 and 312 respectively) compared to Edgewonk (40), Tradervue (9), and Trademetria (5).

How often are Trustpilot ratings updated?

Trustpilot ratings update in real time as new reviews are submitted. However, the aggregate score can be slow to shift for platforms with large review counts. For TradeZella at 835 reviews, a single new one-star review would change the average by less than 0.01 points. For Tradervue at 9 reviews, a single five-star review would raise the average by roughly 0.27 points. This is why review volume matters: large sample sizes produce more stable, trustworthy ratings. We collected all data for this analysis in March 2026 and plan to refresh it annually.

Should Trustpilot be the only factor when choosing a trading journal?

No. Trustpilot measures user sentiment, which is heavily influenced by customer support quality (93% of TradeZella's positive reviews mention support). It does not measure feature depth, analytical capability, or how well a journal improves your actual trading. A platform could have excellent support and high Trustpilot ratings while lacking features you need. Use Trustpilot as one input alongside hands-on testing (most platforms offer free trials), feature comparisons, and independent reviews. Our TJ Score system evaluates 8 categories including broker support, analytics, usability, and pricing to give a more complete picture than Trustpilot alone.

Which trading journal has the most Trustpilot reviews?

TradeZella leads with 835 reviews (4.8/5 average), followed by TraderSync with 312 reviews (4.7/5). Edgewonk has 40 reviews (4.7/5), Tradervue has 9 reviews (2.6/5), and Trademetria has 5 reviews (4.0/5). Higher review counts provide more statistically reliable ratings. TradeZella's 835-review sample is large enough that individual outlier reviews have negligible impact on the overall score.

What do traders complain about most in negative reviews?

The most common complaints vary by platform. For TradeZella: pricing (no free trial) and missing mobile app. For TraderSync: data accuracy issues and sync delays. For Edgewonk: learning curve and limited customer support. For Tradervue: outdated interface and lack of modern features. For Trademetria: limited analytics depth on lower-tier plans. Customer support quality is the strongest predictor of overall satisfaction across all platforms.

How does TradeZella maintain a 4.8 rating with 835 reviews?

Two factors stand out in the review data. First, 93% of positive TradeZella reviews specifically mention customer support quality, suggesting the team actively resolves issues before they become negative reviews. Second, the platform's all-inclusive pricing (every feature available on the Basic plan) reduces friction that typically generates complaints on platforms with tiered feature access. The 4.8 average across 835 reviews is statistically significant and difficult to sustain without genuine user satisfaction.

Is a 4.8 Trustpilot rating good for a trading software platform?

Yes. For context, the average Trustpilot rating across all businesses is approximately 4.2/5. Financial software platforms typically average lower due to the complexity of the products and higher user expectations. A 4.8/5 rating with 835+ reviews places TradeZella in the top tier of financial software on Trustpilot. TraderSync and Edgewonk at 4.7/5 are also well above average, though Edgewonk's smaller sample size (40 reviews) makes that rating less stable.

How should I weigh Trustpilot ratings vs. feature comparisons when choosing a journal?

Use Trustpilot to gauge reliability and support quality, then use feature comparisons to match a platform to your specific needs. A journal with a 4.8 rating but no trade replay is the wrong choice if trade replay is important to you. Conversely, a feature-rich journal with a 2.6 rating may have usability or support problems that offset its capabilities. Our recommendation: narrow your choices by features first, then use Trustpilot and our TJ Scores to pick between the finalists.
