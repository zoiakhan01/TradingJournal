What Is a Trading Journal?

A trading journal is software that records, organizes, and analyzes every trade you make. At its simplest, it's a log of entries and exits with profit/loss calculations. But modern trading journals go far beyond that — they auto-import trades directly from your broker, use AI to detect patterns in your behavior, offer backtesting so you can test strategies against historical data, and provide trade replay so you can rewatch your executions tick by tick.

The difference between a spreadsheet and dedicated journal software comes down to automation and insight. A spreadsheet requires manual entry for every trade (which most traders eventually abandon) and can't generate the kind of multi-dimensional analytics — like correlating your emotional state with P&L, or identifying which setups perform best at specific times of day — that purpose-built platforms provide automatically.

If you're evaluating whether dedicated software is worth the cost, consider this: the average trader who manually journals in a spreadsheet logs trades for about 3 months before quitting. Automated import removes the biggest barrier to consistency. If you want to start with a spreadsheet first, we offer a free trading journal template at the bottom of this page.

3. Edgewonk — Best for Psychology Tracking

TJ Score: 7.0/10  |  Price: $197/year (~$16/month)  |  60+ Importers

Edgewonk takes a fundamentally different approach — it's built around the thesis that most traders lose because of psychology, not strategy. If you believe emotional discipline is your biggest challenge, Edgewonk is the most purposefully designed tool for addressing it.

Visit Edgewonk →

What makes Edgewonk stand out

The Tiltmeter is Edgewonk's signature feature and the only dedicated emotional tracking tool in any trading journal. You rate your emotional state during each trade, and the Tiltmeter correlates those ratings directly with P&L. It quantifies exactly how much money your emotions are costing or earning you — data that no other platform provides.

Edge Finder AI scans your trade log and identifies which variables have the strongest statistical impact on your results. Built on over a decade of data from thousands of trader journals, it reveals where your actual edge comes from.

Coin Flip Distribution compares your results to random chance, proving whether your edge is statistically real or just luck. Edgewonk also offers backtesting against your own trade history — you can simulate how different exit strategies, position sizes, or rule changes would have affected your actual past trades. This isn't the same as backtesting against historical market data (like TradeZella or TraderSync offer), but it's useful for optimizing your existing approach.

At $197/year (~$16/month), it's the cheapest premium journal on the market with a 14-day free trial. The single pricing tier means you get every feature without worrying about plan limitations.

The drawback: No trade replay, no strategy templates, no mobile app, no education, no mentor tools, and only 60+ importers. Backtesting is limited to your own trade history (no historical market data). Edgewonk is a specialized psychology tool, not a comprehensive platform.

Best for: Psychology-focused traders with proven strategies who lose money due to emotional decisions.
