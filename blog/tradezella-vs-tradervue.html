Overview

TradeZella and Tradervue sit on opposite ends of the trading journal evolution. TradeZella represents the modern all-in-one approach — combining journaling, advanced analytics, backtesting with 11+ years of data, trade replay, strategy templates, education, and mentor tools in a single platform. Tradervue, founded in 2011, is one of the original trading journals and takes a simpler, community-oriented approach.

The numbers tell part of the story: TradeZella holds a 4.8/5 Trustpilot rating from 800+ reviews. Tradervue has a 2.6/5 Trustpilot rating. That gap reflects both feature depth and user expectations — in 2026, traders expect more from their tools than basic trade logging, and Tradervue's development pace hasn't kept up with modern demands.

That said, Tradervue has served over 200,000 traders and still offers a functional free plan. Its community-first approach and mentor-sharing features have genuine value. The question is whether those advantages outweigh the substantial feature gap.

Tradervue's free plan is its strongest selling point — 30 trades per month at no cost. For casual swing traders who take a handful of trades monthly, that's a legitimate option for basic trade logging.

But the comparison changes dramatically at paid tiers. At $29/mo (TradeZella Basic) vs. $29.95/mo (Tradervue Silver), the feature gap is enormous. TradeZella Basic includes 50+ analytics reports, trade replay (all 3 modes), unlimited backtesting with 11+ years of data, strategy templates, Zella University, Spaces mentor tools, and Zella Insights AI. Tradervue Silver offers unlimited trade logging, basic reports, and sharing.

At the Gold level ($49.95/mo), Tradervue adds auto-import and risk analysis — features that TradeZella includes on its Basic plan. You're paying $49.95/mo for Tradervue Gold and getting a fraction of what TradeZella Basic delivers at $29/mo.

Winner: Tradervue for the free plan. TradeZella at every paid tier — dramatically more features at comparable or lower prices.

2. Journaling & Trade Import

TradeZella connects to 500+ brokers — Interactive Brokers, MetaTrader 4/5, cTrader, TradeLocker, NinjaTrader, Tradovate, ThinkorSwim, Schwab, Robinhood, and hundreds more. Trades sync automatically. The platform supports 5 asset classes including crypto.

The journaling goes deep: tags and custom categories feeding into 50+ reports, an advanced global filter, a built-in notebook with templates, MFA/MFE statistics, Zella Scale, Best Exit Analysis, and risk management tools with automatic R-multiple calculation.

Tradervue connects to 80+ brokers — a much smaller network that still covers major US platforms but misses many international brokers and newer platforms. Auto-import is only available on the Gold plan ($49.95/mo); Silver users must upload CSV files manually. Tradervue supports 4 asset classes — notably excluding crypto.

The journaling is functional but basic. You can log trades, add notes, and tag setups. TradingView integration automatically generates charts with entry/exit points plotted. Commission tracking is available on paid tiers.

The difference is stark: 500+ brokers vs. 80+. Five asset classes vs. four. Deep analytics tools (MFA/MFE, Zella Scale, Best Exit Analysis) vs. basic logging. Auto-import on all plans vs. Gold-only.

Winner: TradeZella — 5x more broker integrations, more asset classes, and significantly deeper journaling tools.

3. Analytics & Reports

TradeZella generates 50+ pre-built analytics reports: date and time analysis (day of week, time of day, session, duration), price and quantity reports (symbol, price level, position size), risk reports (R-multiple, risk-adjusted returns), options expiration reports, wins vs. losses, custom tag reports, and a Compare feature. Every custom tag automatically generates its own performance report.

Tradervue provides basic reports covering day of week, time of day, trade duration, instrument performance, and tag-based analysis. The reports are clear and readable but limited in scope — roughly 6–8 report types vs. TradeZella's 50+. More detailed analytics require the Silver ($29.95/mo) or Gold ($49.95/mo) tier.

For traders trying to discover patterns in their performance — understanding whether they're more profitable on certain days, with certain position sizes, during certain sessions — TradeZella's 50+ reports provide dramatically more ways to interrogate your data.

Winner: TradeZella — this isn't close. 50+ structured reports vs. a handful of basic breakdowns.

4. AI & Smart Features

TradeZella includes Zella Insights — data-driven pattern recognition that identifies factors driving your P&L, surfaces risk concentration warnings, and analyzes behavioral patterns. Zella AI (coming soon) will add conversational capabilities for asking questions about your trading and getting personalized insights.

Tradervue has no AI features. No pattern recognition, no automated insights, no smart analysis. All interpretation is manual.

In 2026, AI-powered analysis is becoming a baseline expectation for trading journals. The ability to have an AI surface patterns you might miss — like the fact that your revenge trades after losses are costing you 3% monthly, or that your win rate drops dramatically in the last hour of trading — is genuinely valuable. Tradervue's absence of any smart features is a notable gap.

Winner: TradeZella — AI-powered insights vs. none.

5. Trade Replay

TradeZella offers Trade Replay on all plans with three modes: Trade Replay (rewatch individual trades second by second), Day Replay (replay your entire trading session to understand sequence and psychology), and Scenario Replay (coming soon — compare similar setups for pattern recognition). You get speed control up to 30x, entry/exit markers, multiple timeframes, Time of Sales, Level II data, and integrated journaling during replay.

Tradervue does not have trade replay. You can view static charts of your trades (generated through TradingView), but you can't step through market data as it unfolded.

Trade replay transforms how you learn from your trades. Instead of looking at a static chart and trying to remember what you were thinking, you can rewatch the market unfold in real time and identify exactly where your decisions went right or wrong. For day traders, it's one of the most effective improvement tools available.

Winner: TradeZella — comprehensive replay system vs. none.

6. Backtesting

TradeZella includes unlimited backtesting on all plans with 11+ years of historical data back to September 2014. The backtesting engine supports multi-symbol testing (up to 5 per session), multi-chart layouts (up to 8 simultaneously), built-in ICT indicators (FVG, Asian Session Range, HTF Bias, Key Levels, Power of 3), realistic execution with market/limit/stop orders, drag-and-drop SL/TP, speed control (0.5x to 10x), economic calendar on chart, Go-To feature for jumping to dates/sessions, and complete integration with strategy templates. Every backtested trade auto-logs to your journal.

Tradervue does not have backtesting.

For traders developing new strategies or validating existing ones against historical data, backtesting is essential. TradeZella's backtesting is comprehensive enough that many traders don't need a separate backtesting platform. This is a major feature gap for Tradervue.

Winner: TradeZella — full backtesting engine vs. none.

7. Strategy Tools

TradeZella provides 25+ ready-made strategy templates from professional traders — covering ICT-based approaches, volume profile, break and retest, auction market theory, and more. You can create custom strategy templates with specific rules, tag trades to strategy templates, track each strategy template's performance, log missed trades (setups you didn't take), and share strategy templates with others.

Tradervue has no strategy management system. You can tag trades by setup type, but there's no framework for documenting strategies, tracking strategy template performance, or accessing professional templates.

For traders still developing their approach, TradeZella's 25+ professional templates provide proven frameworks to study and adapt. For experienced traders, the strategy template performance tracking shows exactly which strategies are working and which aren't — data that's invisible without structured strategy management.

Winner: TradeZella — complete strategy ecosystem vs. basic tagging.

8. Community & Mentorship

Both platforms emphasize community, but in different ways.

TradeZella:

  • Spaces (Mentor Mode): Full mentorship platform where mentors can monitor up to 5 students simultaneously with color-coded P&L, access students' full dashboards (Trade Log, Notebook, Strategy Templates, Reports), leave targeted feedback on specific trades, create organized coaching folders, and manage unlimited students with no per-seat fees
  • Built-in community features
  • Shared strategy templates for collaborative learning

Tradervue:

  • Trade sharing: Publish trades to the community or share privately with specific people
  • Mentor access: Give a mentor read access to your journal
  • Community forums: Browse and comment on other traders' shared trades
  • Message boards: Topic-based discussions

Tradervue's community was pioneering in 2011 and still functions as a place for traders to share and discuss trades. The mentor-sharing feature works, though it's basic — mentors can view your trades but don't have the analytics, feedback tools, or structured coaching interface that TradeZella's Spaces provides.

TradeZella's Spaces is a professional mentorship platform. The color-coded P&L monitoring, full account access, targeted trade feedback, and unlimited student capacity make it a tool designed for serious coaching relationships — not just sharing trades.

Winner: TradeZella — Spaces is a structured mentorship platform; Tradervue's sharing is functional but limited. Tradervue gets credit for its established open community.

9. Education & Learning

TradeZella includes Zella University — structured learning bootcamps from beginner to advanced, trader development webinars, lessons on recovering from trading losses, and content covering journaling best practices, risk management, and trading psychology. All included in every paid plan at no extra cost.

Tradervue has no education platform. No courses, no webinars, no structured learning content.

For traders who want to improve their skills alongside tracking trades, Zella University adds meaningful value — especially for intermediate traders working to develop consistency.

Winner: TradeZella — full education platform vs. none.

10. User Experience & Interface

TradeZella features a modern, clean interface designed for the current generation of web applications. Navigation between journaling, analytics, backtesting, and education feels intuitive. The customizable dashboard lets you arrange widgets to match your workflow. The overall experience reflects a platform built with modern design standards.

Tradervue was designed in 2011 and looks it. The interface is functional but dated — reminiscent of web applications from a previous era. For users who prioritize aesthetics and modern UX, Tradervue's interface can feel like a step backward. The 2.6/5 Trustpilot rating partly reflects user frustration with the platform's pace of updates and modernization.

That said, Tradervue's simplicity can be an advantage for traders who want straightforward trade logging without complexity. If you just need to import trades and look at basic stats, the no-frills interface gets the job done.

Winner: TradeZella — modern design and UX vs. a dated interface that hasn't kept pace with current standards.

Final Verdict

TradeZella is the better trading journal by a wide margin in 2026.

The comparison is lopsided because these platforms serve different eras of trading technology. TradeZella offers backtesting, trade replay, AI analytics, 25+ strategy templates, Zella University, Spaces mentor tools, built-in ICT indicators, MFA/MFE, Best Exit Analysis, and 50+ reports. Tradervue offers trade logging, basic reports, and community sharing. TradeZella connects to 500+ brokers; Tradervue connects to 80+. TradeZella supports crypto; Tradervue doesn't.

For every other use case — day trading, active trading, strategy development, backtesting, trade review, mentorship, education, or serious performance improvement — TradeZella is the clear choice.

FAQ

Is Tradervue still worth using in 2026?

The free plan is still worth it for casual traders who need basic trade logging at no cost. At paid tiers ($29.95–$49.95/mo), Tradervue is difficult to recommend when TradeZella offers dramatically more features at the same or lower prices.

Does Tradervue have a free plan?

Yes — 30 trades per month for free, including basic import and community access. This is Tradervue's strongest advantage and a genuine option for low-frequency traders.

Which is better for day trading?

TradeZella, by a significant margin. Day traders benefit from trade replay (reviewing execution), backtesting (testing strategies), 50+ analytics reports (identifying patterns), and strategy templates (documenting setups). Tradervue offers none of these features.

Does Tradervue support crypto?

No. Tradervue supports stocks, options, futures, and forex. If you trade crypto, TradeZella is among the platforms that support it with 500+ broker integrations.

Which trading journal has backtesting?

TradeZella includes unlimited backtesting on all plans with 11+ years of historical data, multi-chart layouts, built-in ICT indicators, and strategy template integration. Tradervue does not have backtesting.

TradeZella vs Tradervue for beginners?

TradeZella is better for beginners who want to learn and improve — Zella University provides structured education, and 25+ strategy templates give beginners proven frameworks to study. Tradervue's free plan is an option for beginners on a tight budget who just need basic logging.

Which has better broker integrations?

TradeZella with 500+ vs. Tradervue's 80+. TradeZella covers over 6x more brokers and platforms.

Can I migrate from Tradervue to TradeZella?

Yes. You can export trade data from Tradervue as CSV and import it into TradeZella. TradeZella also supports direct broker connections, so your future trades will sync automatically.

Which trading journal has trade replay?

TradeZella offers trade replay on all plans with three modes — Trade Replay, Day Replay, and Scenario Replay (coming soon). Tradervue does not have trade replay.

Is TradeZella worth the cost vs. free Tradervue?

If you trade actively and want to improve, yes. TradeZella's backtesting, trade replay, 50+ reports, strategy templates, and education tools provide value that basic trade logging can't match. If you only need a free record of your trades, Tradervue's free tier is fine.

Which is better for options trading?

TradeZella. It includes dedicated options expiration reports, strategy templates for options strategies, and backtesting capabilities. Tradervue supports options as an asset class but offers no options-specific analytics or tools.

Does Tradervue have AI features?

No. Tradervue has no AI-powered analysis, no pattern recognition, and no automated insights. TradeZella offers Zella Insights with behavioral analysis and has Zella AI (conversational) coming soon.

Why does Tradervue have a low Trustpilot rating?

Tradervue's 2.6/5 Trustpilot rating reflects user concerns about slow development, a dated interface, and a feature set that hasn't kept pace with modern competitors. The platform still functions for basic trade logging but has fallen behind in analytics, AI, and advanced features.

Does either platform have a mobile app?

Neither has a native mobile app currently. TradeZella has one listed as "coming soon" and offers a responsive web experience. Tradervue is desktop-focused with limited mobile functionality.
