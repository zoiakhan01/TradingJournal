Tradervue Review 2026: The Original Trading Journal — Still Worth It?

March 2026 · 10 min read · By the TradingJournal.com team

Quick Verdict

Tradervue is one of the original trading journal platforms, established in 2011 with over 200,000 traders. It offers a legitimate free plan and a strong community with built-in sharing and mentorship features. However, a 2.6/5 Trustpilot rating, dated interface, and absence of modern features like AI analysis, trade replay, and backtesting place it firmly behind the competition in 2026.

Best for: Budget traders who value community and mentorship over advanced features

Price range: Free -- $49.95/month

Verdict: A serviceable free journal with good community features, but hard to recommend over more modern alternatives at paid tiers unless free is your only option.

What Is Tradervue?

Tradervue is a web-based trading journal founded in 2011 by Greg Reinacker, the former founder and CTO of NewsGator. After building the tool for his own use, he released it publicly and it became one of the first dedicated trading journal platforms on the market.

The platform serves over 200,000 traders and takes a community-first approach -- emphasizing peer mentorship, shared learning through message boards, and collaboration. It integrates with 80+ brokers and uses TradingView for automatic chart generation with entry/exit points.

At 15 years old, however, Tradervue shows its age. The interface feels dated, development has slowed, and it lacks the AI, replay, and backtesting features that define modern journals.

Key Features

Free Plan (30 Trades/Month)

Tradervue's free tier allows up to 30 trades per month with basic import from supported brokers. For casual swing traders or those evaluating the platform, this is a no-risk way to test. The free plan includes community access and basic charting.

This is Tradervue's strongest advantage -- no other major trading journal offers a comparable free plan. Trademetria offers a free tier as well (30 orders/month), but Tradervue's community features on the free plan add more value.

Automatic Chart Generation

The platform's most practical feature: TradingView integration automatically generates price charts for each trade with your entry and exit points plotted. Charts display across multiple timeframes (1-minute to weekly), and you can add indicators, trend lines, and annotations.

This saves the tedious work of taking manual screenshots for every trade.

Reports & Analysis

Tradervue provides basic reports covering day of week performance, time of day analysis, trade duration, instrument performance, tag-based analysis, and win/loss breakdowns. The reports are clear and functional but limited in scope -- roughly 6--8 report types.

For comparison, platforms like TradeZella (with 500+ broker integrations) offer 50+ pre-built analytics reports with custom tag reports, compare tools, and options expiration analysis. Tradervue's analytics feel like a snapshot from a previous generation of journal software.

Community & Sharing

This is where Tradervue's legacy shines. The platform has an active community of traders who share trades publicly, discuss setups on message boards, and offer peer mentorship. You can publish trades to the community, share privately with specific people, or give a mentor read access to your journal.

For traders who learn through peer interaction and community discussion, Tradervue's sharing model has genuine value.

Commission Tracking

Available on paid tiers, commission tracking automatically calculates the impact of commissions and fees on your P&L -- a practical feature for active traders who need to understand their true net performance.

For the same price, TradeZella Basic ($29/mo) includes 50+ reports, backtesting with 11+ years of data, trade replay, 25+ strategy templates, Zella University education, and Spaces mentor tools. The feature gap at comparable prices is enormous.

Tradervue Gold at $49.95/mo adds auto-import and risk analysis -- features included on TradeZella's Basic plan. You're paying premium prices for basic functionality.

How Tradervue Compares

Tradervue pioneered the trading journal category in 2011 and deserves credit for that. But the market has moved significantly forward. Modern platforms like TradeZella (with 500+ broker integrations) offer backtesting, trade replay, AI analytics, strategy templates, education, and mentor tools -- none of which Tradervue has. Even at the free tier, Trademetria matches Tradervue's 30-order limit while offering mobile apps and more modern analytics.

The only scenario where Tradervue clearly wins is for traders who want a zero-cost journal with community features and have no need for advanced tools. Once you're willing to pay anything, the value proposition collapses against modern competitors.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Tradervue still worth using in 2026?

The free plan is still a legitimate option for casual traders who need basic logging at no cost. At paid tiers, it's difficult to recommend when modern platforms offer dramatically more features at comparable or lower prices.

Does Tradervue have a free plan?

Yes -- 30 trades per month for free, including basic import and community access. This is Tradervue's strongest selling point.

Does Tradervue support crypto?

No. Tradervue supports stocks, options, futures, and forex only.

Does Tradervue have backtesting or trade replay?

No to both. Tradervue has no backtesting and no trade replay capabilities.

Why does Tradervue have a low Trustpilot rating?

The 2.6/5 rating reflects concerns about slow development, a dated interface, and features that haven't kept pace with modern competitors. Users expect more from paid journal software in 2026.

How many brokers does Tradervue support?

80+ -- covering major US platforms but significantly fewer than TradeZella (500+) or TraderSync (900+).

Does Tradervue have a mobile app?

No. The platform is desktop-focused with limited mobile functionality.

Can I migrate from Tradervue to another journal?

Yes. You can export trade data as CSV and import it into platforms like TradeZella, which also supports direct broker connections for automatic syncing going forward.

Is Tradervue good for beginners?

The free plan is a low-risk option for beginners. But for learning and development, platforms with education features (like TradeZella's Zella University and 25+ strategy templates) provide more structured support.

Does Tradervue have AI features?

No. Tradervue has no AI-powered analysis, no pattern recognition, and no automated insights.
